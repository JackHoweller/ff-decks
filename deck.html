Agile methodology
An iterative process develops a project in small increments, with continuous feedback and improvement based on each cycle.
Agile relies on this approach - breaking work into defined sprints with clear goals, reviews, and retrospectives at each stage.
Sprint
A short, time-boxed period when a team works to complete a set amount of work.
Typical duration: 1 week early on → 2 weeks as complexity grows → longer as the product matures.
As you build, your model learns - every human correction is a labelled training example

Data
Management
Your data flywheel is the gap between what your model predicts and what your experts actually do. Log it, retrain on it - and watch predictions converge on human judgement.
The AI data flywheel
01
Define the delta
Where does your model diverge from what human experts actually do? That gap - override rate, edit rate, correction frequency - is your objective.
→
02
Target convergence
What does closing the gap look like? Set a measurable convergence signal - e.g. override rate drops from 40% to under 10%.
→
03
Assess current state
Where is data today? Constraints: hosting, streaming, tooling, regulations.
04
Log the delta
Every prediction a human overrides or corrects is a labelled training example. Design your system to capture these disagreements automatically.
→
05
Retrain on disagreements
Use the gap between model output and human action as your training set. Weekly retraining cycles compound improvements without anyone manually labelling data.
→
→
Delta shrinks
Model output converges on expert judgement. Each retraining cycle compounds your moat - not just speed, but accumulated domain knowledge no competitor can buy.
The flywheel compounds - each human correction today makes tomorrow's model smarter than any competitor who isn't collecting this signal
